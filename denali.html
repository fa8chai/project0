Denali

Denali
The Great One

Denali (also known as Mount McKinley, its former official name) is the highest mountain peak in North America, with a summit elevation of 20,310 feet (6,190 m) above sea level.

Read More

The first ascent of the main summit of Denali came on June 7, 1913, by a party led by Hudson Stuck and Harry Karstens. The first man to reach the summit was Walter Harper, an Alaska Native. Robert Tatum also made the summit. Using the mountain's contemporary name, Tatum later commented, "The view from the top of Mount McKinley is like looking out the windows of Heaven!"

Facts :
  • The upper half of Denali is permanently covered with snow and has five massive glaciers on its slopes.
  • In 1903, James Wickersham recorded the first attempt at climbing Denali, which was unsuccessful.
  • Located in the Alaska Range in the interior of the U.S. state of Alaska
